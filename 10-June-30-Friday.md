## June 30th, Friday
### Tokyo, Day One of Exploration

**Today's itinerary (according to Dad, who made a spreadsheet):**

Morning: Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa
Afternoon: Akihabara (for Tilly, who is obsessed with Japanese stationery)
Evening: Shibuya Crossing, because apparently it's famous

**What Actually Happened:**

Senso-ji was beautiful. Like, actually beautiful. This old temple with a massive red gate (called a "Thunder Gate," how cool is that) and a long street leading up to it full of shops selling snacks and souvenirs and traditional stuff.

I bought a fortune there. You put in coins, shake a box, and get a stick with a number, and then you find the drawer with your fortune.

Mine said something about "patience will bring reward" and "avoid travel in the north direction." I don't know where north is but I'm going to try to avoid it just in case.

Mum's fortune was bad. Like, actually bad. The word for bad fortune is "kyo" apparently and when she opened it she went "Oh no." Dad said it's fine, it's superstition, it doesn't mean anything.

You're supposed to tie bad fortunes to a special rack at the temple so the bad luck stays there instead of following you.

Mum tied hers very carefully.

Akihabara was INSANE. Buildings and buildings of electronics, anime stuff, games, and yes, stationery. Tilly went feral in the stationery store. She bought approximately fourteen different pens, erasers shaped like food, sticky notes with cats on them, and a notebook with a very serious-looking cartoon rabbit on the cover.

I bought a pen that has a little cat on the end that bobbles when you write.

No regrets.

Shibuya Crossing at night is like nothing I've ever seen. You stand on the corner with hundreds of other people and then ALL the lights turn red and EVERYONE crosses at once, like a human flood, going in every direction.

Tilly held my hand, which she hasn't done in a while. I think she was scared but didn't want to admit it.

I was a little scared too.

But also exhilarated.
