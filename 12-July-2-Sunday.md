## July 2nd, Sunday
### Tokyo Tower, 11:00 AM

We went up Tokyo Tower, which is like a red and white Eiffel Tower, and looked at the whole city.

It goes on FOREVER.

I genuinely couldn't see where it ended. Just buildings and buildings and buildings.

Mum said Tokyo is one of the biggest cities in the world. I believe it.

From up there, all the people looked like ants. All these tiny ant-people with their tiny ant-lives, going to work and going home and living.

I had a weird moment looking down.

I thought: all these people, millions of them, and none of them know I exist.

But also, before this trip, I didn't know any of them existed either. Not really. Japan was just a concept. A place on a map. And now I'm here, ant-sized, part of it.

It made me feel small and big at the same time.

### A Cafe in Roppongi, 4:00 PM

Dad is in architecture heaven. We went to this neighborhood with a famous modern building called the Roppongi Hills Mori Tower and he took approximately 47 photos of the design and kept saying "Look at the cantilevers!"

I don't know what cantilevers are but he's happy.

Mum and Sofia and I found a cafe and we're eating matcha cake (GREEN CAKE) and waiting for him to finish his architectural appreciation.

Sofia is drawing in her new notebook. She's drawing a cat wearing a kimono, which she saw in a shop somewhere.

Mum is on her phone, texting Lola updates.

And I'm writing this.

It's quiet. Peaceful.

I like Japan.

It's not like the Philippines at all. It's not like London either. It's its own thing.

Maybe that's okay. Maybe I can be connected to places that are different from each other. Maybe I don't have to choose.
