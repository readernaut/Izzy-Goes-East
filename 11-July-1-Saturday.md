## July 1st, Saturday
### Harajuku, 2:00 PM

Today we went to Harajuku, which is the fashion district. Mum said it's where young people come to express themselves.

I don't know what I expected. Something weird, probably.

But actually it was just... cool. People wearing whatever they wanted. Colorful hair. Unique outfits. Nobody staring at anybody else in a judgmental way.

In London, if you wore something really out there, people would look. Maybe not say anything (British politeness) but definitely look.

Here people were wearing literal costumes and nobody blinked.

Sofia wanted to buy a rainbow tutu from one of the shops. Dad said no. She negotiated down to rainbow socks. This is called compromise, apparently.

We also went to this place called Takeshita Street, which is narrow and packed with people and shops selling crepes and accessories and clothes.

I got a crepe with strawberries and whipped cream and chocolate and I ate it while we walked and it was messy and perfect.

**Note to self:** Tokyo crepes are a valid life choice.

---

### Ueno Park, 5:00 PM

We walked through this huge park in the city center. There were temples, museums, a zoo (we didn't go in, Sofia was still upset about the tutu).

At one point I sat on a bench while Mum and Dad looked at some old temple thing and Sofia chased pigeons.

A girl sat on the other end of the bench. Maybe a little older than me, twelve or thirteen. She had a sketchbook and was drawing.

We didn't talk. We didn't even look at each other.

But after a few minutes she showed me her drawing. It was the temple, really detailed.

"Sugoi," I said, which is basically the only Japanese word I know. It means "amazing" or "cool."

She smiled. "Arigatou."

And that was it. She went back to drawing. I went back to sitting.

But it felt like a connection, somehow. Two people, not speaking the same language, sharing a moment.

Maybe that's what travel is. Little moments that don't mean much but also mean everything.

### Hotel, 10:00 PM

**Things I've noticed about Japan (so far):**

1. It's SO clean. Like, weirdly clean. No trash anywhere.
2. People are polite but reserved. Not cold, just... private.
3. Vending machines EVERYWHERE. For drinks, snacks, hot coffee, cold coffee, soup (?!), all of it.
4. The convenience stores (called "konbinis") are actually incredible. Fresh food, good onigiri (rice balls), hot food, everything.
5. Public transport is silent. Nobody talks on the train. It's kind of peaceful.
6. Everything is cute. Even official things have mascots. There's a mascot for the subway. A mascot for the police. A mascot for literal government agencies.
7. It's expensive but also not? Like, trains and food are similar to London, but some things are way cheaper.
