## June 23rd, Friday
### Heathrow Airport, Gate 47, 3:22 PM

Mum bought me this journal so I could "capture the adventure." Her exact words. She said it with the kind of smile that meant *this is not optional*.

So here I am. Capturing.

**Things I have captured so far:**

- We have been at this gate for 47 minutes
- Tilly has asked "when are we boarding" eleven times
- Dad is doing his crossword and pretending not to hear
- Mum is on her phone texting approximately every relative in the Philippines to confirm we are coming (they know, Mum, they definitely know)
- There is a toddler three rows over who has been screaming since we sat down and honestly? Same, little guy. Same.

Thirteen hours to Manila.

THIRTEEN HOURS.

I did the maths and that's like watching the entire Lord of the Rings trilogy twice. Including the boring bits.

Mum keeps saying I'm going to love it, that I'll finally get to really meet everyone, that it'll be "so good for the girls to connect with their heritage."

She says "heritage" the way some people say "vegetables." Like it's good for you. Like you should just eat it and stop complaining.

I don't even know what I'm supposed to connect WITH. I've seen photos. I've eaten the food. I know the names: Lolo, Lola, Tita Nene, Tita Gemma, Tito Boy. But knowing names isn't the same as knowing people.

What if they're disappointed?

What if I'm the wrong kind of Filipino?

What if they expect me to know stuff and I just... don't?

Okay. The toddler has stopped screaming. I think that's my cue to stop spiraling.

Tilly just asked when we're boarding.

Twelve.

### Same Gate, 3:58 PM

Roshni texted me.

**Roshni:** how's it going
**Me:** still at the gate. dying slowly.
**Roshni:** haha you'll be fine
**Roshni:** send me pics of everything ok??
**Me:** will try to remember between the jet lag and the constant fear
**Roshni:** drama queen
**Roshni:** you're going to have the BEST time
**Roshni:** and eat the best food
**Roshni:** I'm so jealous
**Me:** you literally went to india last year
**Roshni:** that's different
**Me:** how is that different
**Roshni:** idk it just is

I don't think she meant anything by it. But it felt weird. Like going to India is normal, expected, a regular thing that British-Indian kids do, and going to the Philippines is... exotic? Special? Different?

Or maybe I'm reading too much into it.

I do that.

### On the Plane, Somewhere Over Eastern Europe Probably, 7:41 PM (London Time)

Things I have learned in the first four hours of this flight:

1. Airplane food is exactly as bad as everyone says
2. Tilly can fall asleep anywhere, anytime, like a cat
3. Dad is already on his third film
4. Mum keeps trying to talk to me about who we're going to see and I keep putting my headphones back in because I cannot handle the Briefing right now
5. The seat in front of me is reclined so far back I can count the dandruff flakes on the headrest

I tried watching a movie but I couldn't concentrate. I kept thinking about what's going to happen when we land.

Here's what I remember from when I was four (which is basically nothing):

- Hot. Like, SO hot.
- Loud. Lots of people talking at once.
- Someone (Lola? Tita Nene?) crying when she saw us
- Eating something sticky and sweet that I later found out was called kalamay
- Sleeping in a room with a fan that made this clicking noise all night

That's it. That's the whole memory bank.

Mum has told me more, obviously. She's told me about the house, about the neighborhood, about everyone who's going to be there. But it's like reading a Wikipedia article about a place. You know facts, but you don't know the PLACE.

I'm going to try to sleep now.

Nine more hours.

Nine.

---

### Still on the Plane, Somewhere Over the Middle East Maybe, 11:23 PM (London Time)

Can't sleep.

The thing is, I don't even look that Filipino.

I know that sounds like a weird thing to think about, but it matters. It matters because when people see me, they don't automatically know what I am. They just know I'm... something. Not fully white, not obviously anything specific.

I get asked "where are you from" A LOT.

The conversation usually goes like this:

"Where are you from?"
"London."
"No, but where are you REALLY from?"
"...London?"
"But like, where are your parents from?"
"My dad's from Bristol and my mum's from the Philippines."
"Oh! I THOUGHT you looked a bit Asian!"

And then they look pleased with themselves, like they solved a puzzle.

Tilly looks more like Mum than I do. She has Mum's darker hair and eyes, the more obvious features. People see her and think "Asian kid." People see me and think "...something?"

I don't know why this bothers me so much.

Actually, I do know.

It's because I feel like I'm failing at this. Like there's a test for being Filipino and I'm not even passing the visual part.

When I get there, will they look at me and think: ah yes, one of us?

Or will they look at me and think: who's this random white-passing girl who doesn't speak Tagalog?

Ugh.

Going to try to sleep again.

Eight more hours.
