## July 8th, Saturday
### Taipei, Exploring with Wendy

Wendy and I went to the bookstore together today. Just the two of us. (Well, Auntie Grace was nearby doing errands but she let us wander.)

Taiwanese bookstores are AMAZING. Multiple floors. Manga sections bigger than anything in London. Art supplies. Stationery.

Wendy bought a manga. I bought a sketchbook (even though I don't really draw, but maybe I'll start?).

We sat at a cafe in the bookstore and talked.

"Do you think you'll move back to LA someday?" I asked.

"Maybe. I don't know. Mom likes it here. I like it here too. But LA is where I was born. It's different."

"Do you feel Taiwanese?"

She thought about it. "Sometimes. When I'm doing Taiwanese things, with my family, eating our food. Other times I feel American. In between, mostly."

"That sounds familiar."

She smiled. "You're in between too?"

"British-Filipino. But I don't speak Tagalog. I've only been to the Philippines once, before this trip. And now I'm here, in Taiwan, which I have no connection to at all, and somehow I feel... connected anyway?"

"Maybe connection isn't about blood," Wendy said. "Maybe it's about paying attention."

That's the wisest thing a nine-year-old has ever said to me.

### Temple Near Auntie Grace's Apartment, 6:00 PM

We went to the famous temple nearby. Longshan Temple.

It's ornate and colorful and full of people praying and burning incense.

Mum said she's not religious but she respects people who are. Dad looked uncomfortable (he's very British about religion, meaning he doesn't talk about it).

Auntie Grace lit some incense and showed us how to hold it and bow.

"You don't have to believe to participate," she said. "It's about respect. For the tradition, for the people who came before."

I lit incense. I didn't pray exactly, because I don't know how, but I thought about Lola, and the trip, and everything I've seen.

I thought: thank you.

To who or what, I don't know.

But it felt right.
