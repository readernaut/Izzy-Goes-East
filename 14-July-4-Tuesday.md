## July 4th, Tuesday
### Last Day in Tokyo

We fly to Taipei tomorrow.

Today was our "free day," meaning no plans, just wander.

We went to a park and just sat. Fed some koi fish. Watched old men playing chess (or whatever the Japanese chess equivalent is, called "shogi" I think).

Tilly fell asleep on a bench. She's been running herself ragged.

Mum sat next to me.

"What do you think of Japan?" she asked.

"I love it," I said. "It's so different from Manila."

"Different how?"

I thought about it. "Manila was... overwhelming. But warm. Everyone in your face, everyone talking to you, everyone feeding you. Japan is... calm. Nobody talks to you. But it's nice too. Just different."

She nodded. "You'll find every place has its own way. Taipei will be different again."

"Do you like it here?"

"I do. But it's not home. Not like the Philippines."

"Is London home?"

She smiled. "London is where my family is. So yes. That's a different kind of home."

I thought about that for the rest of the afternoon.
