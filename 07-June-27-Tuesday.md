## June 27th, Tuesday
### Morning, Lola's Kitchen

Something happened at breakfast.

Tita Nene was talking to Mum in Tagalog, fast, and they were laughing, and I was sitting there understanding about one word in ten.

And I got frustrated.

Not angry, exactly. But frustrated. Left out.

Mum must have noticed because she stopped and said, "Sorry, anak. We were just talking about when we were girls."

"I know," I said. "I just wish I could understand."

The table got quiet. Lola looked at Mum with this expression I couldn't read.

Then Tita Nene said, in English: "It's okay. We can teach you, if you want."

"Yes," Lola said. "We have a few days still. We can teach."

And Mum looked at me, really looked at me, and I saw something in her face. Guilt? Hope? Regret?

"I should have taught you earlier," she said quietly. "I thought... in London, I thought it wasn't important. That you'd learn later. I was wrong."

I didn't know what to say.

"It's not too late," Lola said gently. To Mum, not to me. "Don't carry that guilt. It's not too late."

### Maya's Room, 12:00 PM

Maya has been teaching me words.

We sat on her bed (she shares a room with Marco, who was off playing somewhere) and she wrote them down in my journal:

**Tagalog Lesson #1 with Maya:**

- Salamat = thank you
- Oo = yes
- Hindi = no
- Maganda = beautiful
- Masarap = delicious
- Gutom = hungry (important, Maya says, for asking for food)
- Mahal = love (but also expensive? the same word? confusing)
- Pamilya = family

"That's weird," I said about mahal. "Love and expensive are the same word?"

Maya grinned. "Love is expensive."

"That's deep."

"I'm a deep person."

I tried saying them out loud and Maya corrected my pronunciation. Tagalog has sounds that don't exist in English, or at least not the same way, and I kept getting them wrong.

"Your Tagalog sounds like Tita Mari's," Maya said.

"Is that bad?"

"No. It's just... diaspora Tagalog. It sounds different. Like Taglish but more... London."

"Tagalog-London? Taglondon?"

She laughed. "Londalog."

I wrote that down too.

---

### Evening, Lola's Porch

I tried saying "salamat" at dinner when Tita Nene passed me food.

Everyone's heads turned like I'd announced I was running for president.

"O!" Tita Gemma said. "Nag-Tagalog!"

"She knows words now," Maya said proudly, like she'd created a science project.

Lola smiled so big I thought her face might crack.

It was just one word. Just "thank you."

But it felt like something.
