## June 28th, Wednesday
### The Beach at Batangas, Time Unknown Because I'm on Vacation

Tito Boy drove us all to the beach today.

Two hours in the van, stuffed with people, coolers of food, mats, umbrellas, the whole production.

When we got there I understood why.

The water is INSANE. This clear, bright turquoise that doesn't look real. White sand. Fish you can see swimming around your feet.

Sofia screamed and ran straight in. Dad looked at the sun like it was personally attacking him and put on approximately seven layers of sunscreen.

Maya and I waded in together. The water was warm, like bathwater, and clear all the way down.

"It's so different from Brighton," I said.

"Brighton?"

"Beach in England. Near London. The water is brown."

"Brown?" She looked horrified.

"Not dirty brown. Just... brown. Cold too. You don't really swim, you just sort of endure it."

Maya shook her head. "Tita Mari married a man from a country with brown water."

"He had other qualities."

"I hope so."

We floated for a while, not talking. The sun was hot but the water was cool and I could feel something unwinding in my chest, some tension I didn't know I was carrying.

Tita Nene called us over for lunch. There were so many containers: rice (of course), fried chicken, pancit, some kind of fish, fruit I didn't recognize, a thermos of this juice called calamansi.

We ate sitting on mats in the shade. Everyone was wet and sandy and happy.

Lola wasn't swimming ("too old, apo, you enjoy") but she was watching from her chair, smiling, occasionally swatting away bugs with her fan.

At one point Mum came and sat next to me.

"Okay?" she asked.

"Yeah."

"Really okay? You can tell me if it's too much."

I thought about it. It was too much, kind of. Overwhelming. Crowded. Loud. Hot.

But also...

"It's good," I said. "I'm glad we came."

She squeezed my hand.

---

### Night, Lola's House, 10:30 PM

Last night in Manila before we fly to Tokyo tomorrow.

I didn't expect to be sad about leaving.

But I am?

I sat with Lola on the porch after dinner and she was quiet for a long time.

"You will come back," she said. It wasn't a question.

"I hope so."

"You will." She nodded, like she'd decided. "Maybe you bring a husband someday. Or girlfriend. Whatever you want. I don't judge."

I almost choked on my calamansi juice.

"Lola!"

"What? I'm modern. I watch Ellen."

I didn't know Ellen was still on in the Philippines but I wasn't going to argue.

"When you come back," she said, "your Tagalog will be better. You will practice, yes?"

"I'll try."

"Good. And Izzy?"

"Yeah?"

"You belong here too. Maybe not the same way your mother does. Maybe different. But you belong. This is also your bayan."

I don't know why that made me tear up.

Balikbayan. Returning to country.

Maybe someday that will be me. Returning. Not discovering for the first time, but coming back to something I know.

**Tagalog Lesson #2 (learned today):**

- Paalam = goodbye (but like, "going now" goodbye)
- Ingat = take care
- Mahal kita = I love you

Lola taught me that last one.

She hugged me at bedtime and said "mahal kita, apo" and I said it back, stumbling over the words, and she cried.

Good tears, Mum said. Happy tears.
