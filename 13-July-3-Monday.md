## July 3rd, Monday
### Kyoto Day Trip, 7:00 AM on the Shinkansen

We took the bullet train to Kyoto today.

THE BULLET TRAIN IS SO FAST.

Like, irresponsibly fast. The countryside is zooming by and I feel like I'm in a video game.

Dad is timing our arrival because the trains here are famous for being exactly on time. He's very excited about this. ("They apologize if the train is more than thirty seconds late!")

Kyoto is apparently the old capital of Japan and has lots of temples and traditional stuff. It's where geishas are from.

More later. Hard to write on a moving train.

---

### Fushimi Inari Shrine, 11:00 AM

OH MY GOD.

There's this shrine that has thousands of red gates going up a mountain. Like, THOUSANDS. You walk through them, one after another after another, and it just keeps going.

The gates are called "torii" and they're donated by businesses and individuals and they have names written on them.

It was hot and there were lots of stairs and at some point Tilly started complaining and Dad carried her on his back, which was impressive.

But it was worth it.

When you get to the top, you can see the whole city. And the walk through the gates is like... I don't know how to describe it. Like walking through a dream. Red and orange and light filtering through.

Mum took a photo of me and Tilly standing in the middle of the gates. We look tiny. Like ants again.

I'm going to frame that photo, I think.

---

### Geisha District (Gion), 3:00 PM

We saw actual geishas.

Well, maiko. Apprentice geishas. They were walking to an appointment or something and they were STUNNING. White makeup, elaborate kimono, the whole thing.

Mum said you're not supposed to bother them or take photos without permission, so I just watched.

They moved so carefully. So elegantly. Like every step was planned.

I tried to walk like that for about thirty seconds and almost tripped over my own feet. Different skill set.

---

### Train Back to Tokyo, 7:00 PM

My feet hurt. My brain hurts. I've seen too many beautiful things and I need to lie down.

But also: today was amazing.

I keep waiting for travel to get boring, for me to stop being impressed by things, but it hasn't happened yet.
