# PART THREE: TOKYO TO TAIPEI

---

## July 5th, Wednesday
### Airplane, Tokyo to Taipei, Somewhere Over the East China Sea

Short flight this time. Like two and a half hours.

I'm getting good at airports.

**Airport skills I have developed:**

1. Knowing which line is shortest
2. Having documents ready
3. Remembering to take belt off before security
4. Filling water bottle AFTER security
5. Finding the best seat at the gate (near outlet, facing departure screen)

I feel very competent.

---

### Taipei Taoyuan Airport, 4:00 PM Local Time

Taiwan feels different already.

Smaller than Japan. Less overwhelming than Manila. Something in between?

The airport is efficient but not quite as robotic as Tokyo. People are friendly but not quite as exuberant as Manila.

We're taking a train to the city and Mum is texting Auntie Grace to coordinate.

**About Auntie Grace (according to Mum):**

- They were roommates in university in San Francisco
- Auntie Grace is Taiwanese-American
- She moved back to Taipei a few years ago
- She has a daughter who's a bit younger than me (nine, so between me and Sofia)
- Her name is Lily
- They live near this famous temple

I'm nervous about meeting more new people but also excited.

This trip has been nothing but meeting new people and it's been... okay, actually. Good, even.

---

### Auntie Grace's Apartment, 8:00 PM

AUNTIE GRACE MADE TAIWANESE FOOD FOR US AND IT'S SO GOOD.

Beef noodle soup. Dumplings called "xiao long bao" that have soup INSIDE them (!!). Pork belly buns. Something called "gua bao" which is like a steamed bun sandwich.

I've eaten so much on this trip. So much.

Auntie Grace is cool. She's loud like Mum's Filipino relatives but in a different way. More... sarcastic? She makes fun of Mum a lot but in a loving way.

"Look at you," she said when she saw us. "So British now. So fancy."

"I'm not fancy," Mum said.

"You're wearing a linen dress. That's fancy."

"It's practical for hot weather!"

"Fancy and practical. Worse."

They laughed and hugged for a long time.

Lily is shy. She hung back while the adults talked, playing with her tablet. Sofia tried to make friends but Lily kept giving one-word answers.

I get it. Sometimes new people are overwhelming.

---

### Before Bed, 11:00 PM

I'm sharing a room with Sofia and Lily. It's a sleepover situation.

Lily hasn't said much but when Sofia fell asleep, she turned to me and said:

"Do you like anime?"

"Some of it."

"Which ones?"

And then we talked for like an hour about anime. She knows SO much. She has opinions about every show, every character, every studio.

Turns out shy doesn't mean quiet, it just means it takes a while to warm up.

"Will you be here for a few days?" she asked.

"A week."

"Good. I can show you the night market."

Her eyes lit up when she said "night market."

I don't know what a night market is but I'm excited to find out.
